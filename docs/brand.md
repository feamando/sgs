## Naming Convention

Model and product names draw from **Berlin's physics heritage** (the city where modern light physics was born) and **German language** — reflecting both the technology's roots in radiance fields and the company's Berlin connection.

### Model Family: Berlin Physicists (Ascending Scale)

| Tier | Name | Namesake | Parameters | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Micro | **Planck** | Max Planck (Berlin, 1900) — quantum theory | ~100M | The quantum: the smallest meaningful unit of light |
| Small | **Hertz** | Heinrich Hertz — electromagnetic waves | ~1B | Waves: transmission, frequency, communication |
| Medium | **Helmholtz** | Hermann von Helmholtz (Berlin) — perception, optics | Future | The grand unifier of perception and physics |
| Large | **Einstein** | Albert Einstein (Berlin, 1915) — general relativity | Future | Everything is relative; everything is connected |

Usage: **"Radiance Planck"**, **"Radiance Hertz"**, etc. — like "Claude Haiku", "Claude Sonnet."

The scale mirrors physics history: Planck discovered the smallest unit of light. Hertz proved light is a wave. Helmholtz unified perception. Einstein unified everything.

### Products

| Product | Name | What | Origin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Visualizer | **Radiance Prisma** | Interactive 3D Gaussian composition demo | German/Latin: a prism decomposes light into components — exactly what the visualizer does for meaning |
| Chat / Generation app | **Radiance Satz** | Text generation interface powered by Planck or Hertz | German: "Satz" = sentence (what it generates), movement in a symphony (Berlin Philharmonic), theorem in mathematics (our Lean proofs). Three meanings, one word. |

## Visual Identity Notes (Future)

- **Color palette:** Warm light spectrum — amber, gold, soft white. Not cold/corporate blue.
- **Logo direction:** A prism or Gaussian blob refracting into spectral colors. Or a minimal "R" formed by overlapping Gaussian ellipses.
- **Typography:** Clean, scientific. Think Nature/Science journal aesthetics, not startup bold.
- **Tone:** Rigorous but accessible. We prove things in Lean 4 and explain them with LEGO analogies.

## Naming Rules

1. **Company:** Always "Radiance Labs" (two words, both capitalized)
2. **Models:** "Radiance [Physicist]" — e.g., "Radiance Planck"
3. **Products:** "Radiance [German word]" — e.g., "Radiance Prisma", "Radiance Satz"
4. **Shorthand:** In context, just the second word — "Planck", "Hertz", "Prisma", "Satz"
5. **Never:** "Radiance AI" (conflicts with other entities)
