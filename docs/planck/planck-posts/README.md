# Planck 1.1 LinkedIn Post Images

Image copies for the four-part LinkedIn series on H-SGS blobs and the
Planck 1.1 validation. Post text lives in `../linkedin_posts.md`.
Naming: `post-{post-number}-{carousel-slot}_{description}.png`.

Generated by `scripts/plot_planck11_posts.py` from
`results/planck11_validation.json`.

## Post 1. The blob concept
- `post-1-1_architecture.png` . H-SGS architecture: base forward pass
  plus parallel blob retrieval plus learned gate. The "built-in RAG"
  framing in one picture.

## Post 2. What we were trying to prove
- `post-2-1_gates.png` . The four-gate validator laid out as a table:
  what Gate 1, 2, 3, and 4a each test and what the pass condition is.

## Post 3. Preliminary results
- `post-3-1_gate_2_3_pass.png` . Three-panel headline: validation loss,
  perplexity (5.76 to 5.30), and mean effective blob weight (0.059) vs
  the 0.05 utilisation floor.

## Post 4. Gate results and Planck 1.3 plan
- `post-4-1_gate_4a_fail.png` . Intra-sample 4-gram repeats per sample:
  Planck 1.0 = 5.16 vs Planck 1.1 = 8.34 (+62%). The real regression.
- `post-4-2_gate_4b_weak_upside.png` . Cross-sample diversity: unique
  4-gram ratio and pairwise Jaccard. The direction is right but the
  magnitude is small.
- `post-4-3_planck13_roadmap.png` . The five-item Planck 1.3 plan,
  ordered by cost and by how directly each attacks Gate 4a.
