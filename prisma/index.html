What is this?

Radiance Prisma visualizes how Semantic Gaussian Splatting (SGS) composes sentence meaning from individual words — using the same rendering equation that 3D Gaussian Splatting uses to render images from overlapping Gaussian blobs.

Each word is a Gaussian distribution in a learned semantic space — a soft blob with a position (meaning), shape (semantic breadth), and opacity (importance). Sentence meaning is computed by alpha-compositing these Gaussians front-to-back:

Meaning(q) = Σᵢ fᵢ · αᵢ · K(q, μᵢ, Σᵢ) · Tᵢ

where K is the Gaussian kernel (how relevant is this word?), α is opacity (how important?), and T is transmittance (how much capacity is left after previous words absorbed their share?).

We've formally proven that this rendering equation is strictly more expressive than the softmax attention used in transformers. Every softmax computation can be exactly replicated by alpha-compositing, but not vice versa.

Click Play to watch words compose left-to-right. Notice how the transmittance bar depletes — early words consume rendering capacity, leaving less for later words (like objects in front blocking those behind in 3D rendering).
